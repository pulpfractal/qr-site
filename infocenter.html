Dear ACCC, I wish to lodge a consumer complaint regarding what appears to be highly suspicious and potentially anti-competitive conduct by a particular AFL team. The conduct follows a repeated pattern. The team creates the impression of genuine competition by starting strongly, building hope among consumers, and in many cases appearing to be in a winning position. However, usually in the second half, the team then allows the other side to regain control and beat them. This pattern is causing significant consumer detriment. Supporters are induced to invest time, emotional energy, merchandise expenditure, and repeated optimism on the apparent representation that the team is competing properly. Yet the recurring second-half collapse suggests the possibility of coordinated conduct, tacit understanding, or some form of collusion with rival teams. I request that the ACCC investigate whether this team is engaging in misleading conduct, unconscionable disappointment, or anti-competitive arrangements that reduce the quality of the football product supplied to long-suffering consumers. Yours sincerely, A concerned football consumer
